The FORGE Method

A 5-step system for solo developers who have too many projects and not enough shipped things.

F
Focus — One project owns the day
Each day, set one project as your Active Mission before you open any editor. The header shows it at all times. You can capture ideas for others, update their context — but you don't build on them today. Pick your mission, commit, execute.
O
One next step — Always defined
Every project must have a single, specific next step — a 30–60 min action, not a vague goal. "Improve the UI" is bad. "Make the sidebar collapse on mobile breakpoint" is good. If you don't know the next step, that IS the next step: figure it out before you close the project.
R
Record context — Before every close
Before ending any session, update "Where I Left Off". Takes 2 minutes, saves 20. Write it to your future self: what state is everything in, what were you just doing, what's the tricky edge case you noticed. This single habit is the difference between stalled and in-progress.
G
Gate new ideas — Park, don't pursue
New ideas feel urgent but they're the #1 killer of momentum. The Parking Lot captures every idea instantly so you honor it without chasing it. Nothing enters Active Projects mid-session without a deliberate decision. Ideas live in the lot until your weekly review.
E
Evaluate weekly — Commit or cut
Every week, spend 15 minutes: for every Stalled project make a binary call — recommit with a next step, or archive it. Check Building projects — are they actually moving? Promote or kill parking lot ideas. Fewer active projects means more shipped things. Ruthlessness is a feature.
